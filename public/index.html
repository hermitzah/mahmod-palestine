Mahmoud's story

Palestine · Displaced father of three

Mahmoud Al-Hassan, 34, was a beloved schoolteacher in northern Gaza. After relentless bombings, his home was reduced to rubble and his school destroyed. He now shelters in a makeshift tent with his children (Yousef 9, Leen 7, and little Lina 4) and his ill mother, Fatima. Lina suffers from severe asthma, without access to proper inhalers.

"Every morning, my kids ask for bread and safety. I refuse to let them lose hope. Your help means the world to us — a warm meal, a blanket, a smile." Every dollar goes directly to verified grassroots aid workers delivering urgent supplies.

  • $18 = fresh food box (rice, oil, vegetables)
  • $45 = asthma medication + medical consultation
  • $80 = school supplies & trauma support for children
  • $160 = winterization kit (blankets, coats, mattresses)
